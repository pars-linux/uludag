Pardus development builds

The purpose of Pardus development builds is to allow developers have the opportunity to see the development status of Pardus Linux. Our distribution has sometimes get criticisms about the short period of time between releases. One day, we sat down and concluded to have periodic builds with the help of Pardusman. So, Pardus weely builds has come out from this idea. This nice piece of software can be used by anyone: It just asks for the PISI database index file, downloads it, and fetches the PISI packages according to the selection criteria. With this tool, you can produce a Pardus media in CD, DVD or even a flash disk, be it 200 Mb or 3 GB in size.

Note that these builds do show the development status of Pardus, that means they are not tested, and not installed on a variety of hardware. Running it means that you may ruin your data, configuration files, e-mails and even all files on your disk. However, we greatly encourage you to install a development build on one of your spare machines and give feedback to Pardus Bugzilla accordingly.

The excitement in this process is that, you'll know all the development progress Pardus Linux will encounter. You'll have access to the latest software and/or technology Pardus brings. This is a good opportunity since you can easily test the unknown, provide feedback and even make amendments in the project lifecycle.

Where can I have development builds?

From time to time, our development builds appear on Pardus servers for testing. These builds can be downloaded from Pardus FTP site. The burning process is the same as a normal CD ISO: Just download it, burn it to a CD-ROM using Nero (Windows) or K3b.

Sending your bug reports

You have downloaded and installed a development build, now what? Test it as much as you can, because quality of Pardus depends on users, and your bug reports will benefit everyone. In order to enter a report or a wish, just point your Firefox to Bugzilla web page and open an account. This is necessary to enter a bug using Bugzilla interface. Then, enter your bug with as much information as possible in a precise manner. One of the developers will be reading your report and act accordingly. Be sure that it is a reproducable problem. Giving all relevant information and anything that the developers might need to reproduce the problem is very important.

Since all source code of Pardus is on the internet, you can download the software, make necessary patches, and send them via Bugzilla or developer's mailing list.
