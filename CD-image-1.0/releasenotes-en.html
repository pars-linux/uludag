Pardus 1.0 Release Notes

Welcome to Pardus, an easy to install and use system, with many enhancements to desktop usability, compatibility and security. With Pardus, you can connect to Internet, read your e-mails, securily communicate with your friends and exploit your computer's multimedia capability by listening to music and watching movies. We kindly invite you to http://www.uludag.org.tr/eng to explore new Pardus opportunities.

Basic system requirements

While Pardus will work on any modest hardware, we recommend that you have 512 Mb of RAM and 1200 Mhz CPU. While these are two very basic conditions to let Pardus work in an effective way, minimum requirements can be set as follows:

256 Mb of RAM (512 Mb recommended)
800 Mhz Intel or AMD CPU (at least 1200 MHz recommended)

We also recommend an internet connection to reach Pardus update services and download many other productivity software.

A sound card will automatically be detected and used after you install Pardus. You will need to configure your printer with a straightforward process in your first login.

What's new in 1.0?

The project developers have been working hard on Pardus for your desktop pleasure. Below, you can find the most notable and interesting new ideas and features of Pardus 1.0.

PiSi: New packaging system

Pardus comes with its own packaging system, PiSi, which has its own characteristics and advantages over other package managers. With PiSi, developers can enjoy packaging new software in an efficient way. End users will get comfortable with PiSi's fast and hassle-free software installation interface. With a few clicks, you can easily search for utilities you need and install it on your disk.

Newest Linux kernel

Pardus comes with 2.6.14 kernel with additional improvements and bug fixes to the stock kernel. Therefore, you will benefit from new hardware drivers and performance enhancements.

OpenOffice.org 2.0 office suite

For your office software needs, Pardus includes an amazing software, OpenOffice.org which can open and save Microsoft Office files. OpenOffice.org's new version, 2.0, has been used widely on both Windows and Linux. You can use OpenOffice.org 2.0's word processor, spreadsheet, presentation and database software without breaking your compatibility with other office files. We have prepared more clip-arts, templates and extensions to this version for your pleasure.

Installation is a snap with YALI

YALI (Yet Another Linux Installer) has been developed and thoroughly tested in Pardus Laboratories, where our distribution is born. New users will find it easy to use: with a few mouse clicks, you can start enjoying Pardus. Not only it can install system in an effective way, one can easily distringush YALI's speed improvements. We believe that YALI is one of the fastest Linux installer on earth.

The K desktop environment 3.5

KDE is the most renowned desktop environment with all-in-one applications and utilities. We have analysed and decided the most comprehensive, yet easy to use KDE components for your desktop experinence. Fore more information, please note down KDE's web site: http://www.kde.org

ÇOMAR, the configuration manager

Pardus comes with its own configuration system, called ÇOMAR, which is a new technology developed in Pardus Laboratories. ÇOMAR configures your ethernet card, video card, monitor and other peripherals in such a way that you can benefit most from your hardware. With ÇOMAR, you won't need extra configuration by hand.

Your new collaboration suite: Kontact

With Kontact, you can read your e-mails, maintain a rich addressbook of your friends, use its built in reminder for your birthdays, anniversaries and meetings, easily connect to your friend's blogs and organize your valuable time with personal information manager. KDE calendar utility (Korganizer) can also organize your calender's synchronization with your colleagues, through a server (Kolab). This way, you can implement a collaboration system in your organization, benefitting...
